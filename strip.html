    • Strip District

    Innovation Center


    1. History

  • Established by James O’Hara and George A. Bayard

  • Its strategic location made it into an industrial center
  • Andrew Carnegie started career in the Strip

  • Now, in present age of technology, Strip transitioning like Pittsburgh as a whole to modern age of technology, with tech companies (ex. Uber) agglomerating in the Strip

    2. New Development and Innovation

  • Near the CBD (Central Business District) of Pittsburgh

  • Connected through the use of a bus system, multiple highways, and a light rail system.

  • Has an effective threshold of 70,000 commuters, but potentially more with expansion of commuting

  • Near many colleges, e.g. CMU and Pitt

  • Offers a safe environment, unlike Hazelwood, with very low crime rates.

  • Many new parks and apartments being constructed


    Out of all 3, the Strip District is the Best Region for a New Tech Company's Headquarters

    There are 3 key reasons for this:

  • 1. The Strip District is undergoing major gentrification and is a prime example of the New Urbanism movement. Its flat geography is a boon to land developers, who need not worry about the hilly region that usually defines Pittsburgh.

  • 2. The Strip is located near the CBD and CMU. This makes it a strategic location for any business.

  • 3. The Strip District is a tolerant and diverse community. It is full of ethnic markets and is ready to innovate and redefine itself, which will be essential if Amazon were to place HQ2 there.
