    • Hazelwood

    Old Heart of Industry


    1. History

  • J&L Steel Co. began to industrialize a strip of land east of the Pittsburgh CBD on the northern bank of the Monongahela River, way back in 1853.

  • Eventually, the expansion stopped, as J&L Steel closed down and declared bankruptcy in 1998, ending Hazelwood's era of growth.

    2. A Gentrifying Area

  • Hazelwood has a population of 6,544 people, and is home to Pittsburgh's recycling plant, Uber's test tracks, and CMU's robotics center.

  • Hazelwood now has prime potential to be the center of a tech company's headquarters, as it has many gentrification movements going on in the area, particularly in the area near Hazelwood deemed “the Waterfront.”

    3. A Poor Neighborhood

  • However, Hazelwood has a generally uneducated population, with only 45% of its population having a bachelor's degree.

  • Nevertheless, with its proximity near places of technology, and a neighborhood that can be gentrified, Hazelwood is a place that has strong development possibilities.
