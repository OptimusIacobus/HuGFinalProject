    • Pittsburgh's General Characteristics

    (And Why It is Great for a Technology Headquarter)

    There are several reasons why technology companies should headquarter in the general area of Pittsburgh.

    Graph showing the dependency ratios of Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, and the United States.

    Young Population

    Pittsburgh is a youthful place full of potential, with a median age of 33.2 years, 24% of the population aged 20-29, and 14% aged 30-39. The dependency ratio is only 37.91% - the national average is 51.76%, as shown in the chart above.

    Stable CBD

    Pittsburgh's Central Business District is growing and prospering, with a population of over 300,000 people in the CBD and over 2.1 million in the general metropolitan area.

    Center of Education

    Of residents over 25, 40.7% have at least a bachelor's degree. Students from far and wide come for the many colleges here in Pittsburgh, such as RMU, Duquesne University, the University of Pittsburgh, and, of course, the greatly renowned Carnegie Mellon University.

    Center of Innovation

    With many new innovative companies at the forefront of technology, Pittsburgh is a hub for new growth in the digital age, making it optimal for the technological basis of Amazon..

    Cheap Housing

    Out of all the cities Amazon is considering, Pittsburgh has a great advantage – cheap housing for the 50,000 workers needed for Amazon. The median home price stands at $129,000 and cost of living is 12% below the national average.

    A Booming Economy

    Pittsburgh has clearly rebounded, as its GDP is significantly higher than that of other cities in the Rust Belt - In 2016, Pittsburgh had a Gross Domestic Product (GDP) of $126.7 billion, while Cleveland and Milwaukee stood at $114.5 billion and $89.2 billion.

    A Comeback City

    After the Industrial Revolution, areas of industrialization including Cleveland, Milwaukee, and Pittsburgh became the “Rust Belt” - yet Pittsburgh has adapted, becoming a leader in the new era of technology.

    Employment Growth

    Pittsburgh's information employment has grown by 2.2%.​

    Pittsburgh's total employment has grown by 1.3%.​

    Over the period of last year, Pittsburgh has gained jobs in nearly all sectors.​

    Focus on Technology

    Pittsburgh is moving from being a industrial center during the Industrial Revolution to becoming a biomedical and technological center, focusing on health services and technology. This can be seen by the dominant jobs in Pittsburgh becoming services in health and tech.
